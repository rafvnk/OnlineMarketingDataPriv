Lookalike Models

Powerful Generated Models

Facebook Pixel

March 22, 2021 by Rafael Von-Klaveren

Lookalike modeling is a smart way advertisers use to narrow down on a group of targeted users who are more aligned into wanting their product or service. A Facebook Pixel does this very well. Let's say if you recently made a website and generated a new pixel on Facebook, you would take the pixel code and paste it into your website. That link is bound to where your ads are getting displayed, that pixel starts off slow, but with more ads that are getting ran and time invested, it slowly begins to learn on which type of audience to target to optimize the efficiency of the ads.

This is what makes these sort of models for big online advertisers. Imagine running a Facebook pixel for a year or two and all the data it would have access to. It can essentially replace the job of someone since it uses actual raw data to narrow the searches. Although it's very helpful towards businesses, users should begin to question on how much data is being grabbed by them to enable these types of models to be generated.
